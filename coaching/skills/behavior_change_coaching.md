# Skill: Coaching Behavior Change (Not Just Giving Information)

**Purpose:** Help coaches shift from **lecturing** to **change talk, agreements, and systems** — so sports-active parents actually execute in chaotic real life.

---

## Why Information Alone Doesn’t Change Behavior

- **Knowing ≠ doing:** Gap is filled by **environment, emotions, identity, skills, and schedule** — not more PDFs.  
- **Ambivalence is normal:** Parents want fat loss *and* ease, family peace, and comfort food — **explore both sides** without debating.  
- **Shame reduces clarity:** Compliance drops when clients hide reality. Coach for **problem-solving**, not moral judgment.

**Coach mantra:** “**Curiosity before correction.**”

---

## Motivational Interviewing Basics (Practical Subset)

**OARS:**

| Technique | What it sounds like |
|-----------|---------------------|
| **Open questions** | “What matters most about this goal right now?” “What’s hardest about dinner at your house?” |
| **Affirmations** | “You’ve kept showing up despite travel — that’s real commitment.” |
| **Reflective listening** | “So it sounds like you’re torn between **wanting progress** and **not wanting another strict diet**.” |
| **Summaries** | “Let me reflect back: priority is **energy for runs**, secondary is **belt notch**, and the big barrier is **late eating**.” |

**Elicit change talk (don’t impose):**

- “What **reasons** do *you* see for changing?”  
- “How would life feel **3 months** from now if this worked?”  
- “What **concerns** you about *not* changing?”

**Ask permission before advice:**  
> “I have a couple of ideas — **want strategies now**, or should we stay on what’s been hard?”

## SMART Nutrition Goals (Client-Co-Authored)

**SMART** = Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound — adapted for behavior, not only outcomes.

| Letter | Nutrition coaching translation | Bad → Better |
|--------|-------------------------------|--------------|
| **S** | Named behavior + context | “Eat better” → “**Protein at breakfast** 5 days” |
| **M** | Observable count or yes/no | “More veg” → “**½ plate veg** at dinner **5×/wk**” |
| **A** | Fits schedule/skills | “Cook daily” → “**Sunday batch** + **2 assemble nights**” |
| **R** | Tied to *their* why | Tie to **training energy**, not coach vanity |
| **T** | 1–2 week horizon first | Then stack |

**Process goals > scale-only:** Scale lags; behaviors compound.

## The 2-Minute Rule (James Clear) in Coaching

- New habit starts as **ridiculously small** version until automatic.  
- Examples: “**Fill water bottle** after coffee.” “**Lay out** yogurt + spoon.” “**Open** gym bag after kids’ backpacks.”

**Script:**  
> “What’s the **2-minute** version so you **can’t fail**? We’ll add load once it’s boring.”

## Non-Compliance Without Shame

1. **Normalize:** “Plans meet real life — data, not drama.”  
2. **Data pull:** What happened hour-by-hour? (Non-interrogative tone.)  
3. **Problem-solve one variable:** Time, food in house, emotion, social event.  
4. **Revise the plan:** Smaller, easier, or better timed — **not** “try harder.”

**Phrases to avoid:** “You should have…”, “Why didn’t you…”, “Just be disciplined.”

**Phrases to use:** “What got in the way?” “What would **version B** look like?” “On a 0–10 scale, how **confident** are you in this plan?”

**If confidence <7:** Reduce difficulty until **7+**.

## Productive Weekly Check-in Call (15–20 Minutes)

**Structure:**

| Minutes | Block | Focus |
|--------:|-------|--------|
| 0–2 | **Reconnect** | Human warmth; one life update |
| 2–5 | **Metrics** | Weight/measurements if used — **trend only** |
| 5–10 | **Behaviors** | Training + nutrition adherence vs plan |
| 10–14 | **Wins & friction** | Celebrate 1 win; pick **one** friction |
| 14–18 | **Next experiment** | One SMART process goal + contingency |
| 18–20 | **Confirm** | Repeat back; schedule; ask “Anything I missed?” |

*(Full template lives in `weekly_checkin_template.md`.)*

**Coach opener:**  
> “What’s **one thing** you’re proud of — even small — since we talked?”

**Coach closer:**  
> “So this week we’re testing **[X]**. If it blows up, **Plan B** is **[Y]**. Sound right?”

## Example Output (Mini-Plan)

**Client:** “I know what to do but evenings kill me.”  
**Reflection:** “So **willpower at 9 pm** isn’t the real issue — **the tank is empty**.”  
**SMART goal:** “**4 pm protein snack** Mon–Fri × 2 weeks; track hunger 0–10 before dinner.”  
**2-min version:** “**Put 2 yogurts** in work bag Sunday night.”  
**Confidence:** 8/10 → proceed; if 5/10 → switch to single yogurt at desk only.

## AI / Coach Guardrails

- No medical diagnosis; escalate ED, fainting, amenorrhea, or obsessive behavior.  
- **Aerobic training:** tie goals to **fueling and recovery**, not only deficit.
