# Skill: Family Meal Prep for Sports-Active Parents

**Purpose:** Give coaches (and AI assistants) a repeatable system so parents hit protein and whole-food targets *without* cooking twice — kids eat the same base meals, parents add simple tweaks (portion, veg volume, pre/post-training carbs).

---

## Step-by-Step Process

1. **Sunday 90-minute block (batch + assemble)**  
   - **Batch cook 2 proteins** (e.g., shredded chicken, turkey/chickpea bolognese).  
   - **Batch cook 1 carb** (rice, potatoes, or pasta — store plain; sauce on the side for kids if needed).  
   - **Wash/chop 2 veg bases** (mixed salad, roasted tray of veg).  
   - **Pre-portion** training-parent snacks (Greek yogurt cups, cheese + fruit, protein bars you approve).

2. **Monday–Wednesday:** Pull from fridge; **Thursday “mini-reset”** (15 min): fresh salad, frozen veg steam, or rotisserie chicken rescue.

3. **Friday:** Freezer meal, leftovers, or agreed takeout with **pre-decided defaults** (see Eating Systems skill).

4. **Assign roles:** One shopper (online cart Friday), one cooker (Sunday), kids on **one** task (set table, wash fruit) for buy-in.

---

## Five High-Protein, Family-Friendly Meals

| Meal | Kid-friendly angle | Parent/training tweak |
|------|--------------------|------------------------|
| **1. Build-a-bowl bar** | Rice/pasta + mild protein + corn/cucumber/cheese toppings | Double protein portion; extra greens; add beans; post-long-run: larger carb portion |
| **2. Turkey or lentil bolognese** | With pasta + parmesan | Parent: half pasta, half zucchini/carrot noodles or extra veg; optional chili flakes adult side |
| **3. Sheet-pan fajitas** | Mild spice; tortillas + mild fixings | Parent: extra chicken/skirt steak or black beans; 2 tortillas if hard training day |
| **4. Homemade “fast food”** | Baked potato or sweet potato + cheese + broccoli | Parent: add tuna, chili, or Greek yogurt + chives for protein |
| **5. Breakfast-for-dinner** | Scrambled eggs, toast, fruit | Parent: 3–4 eggs or egg + egg whites + cottage cheese side; whole-grain toast |

**Batch notes:** Bolognese and fajitas freeze well; bowls and breakfast-for-dinner are fastest assembly nights.

## Handling “I Don’t Have Time to Cook”

**Root causes (diagnose in one question each):**  
- *Skill gap* → teach one “hero” recipe only.  
- *Decision fatigue* → fixed weekly menu (same 5 meals on rotation).  
- *Schedule clash* → shift prep to different day or split (protein only Sunday).  
- *Perfectionism* → “good enough” standard: **protein + veg + whatever carb is available.**

**Script (coach):**  
> “We’re not aiming for Pinterest. We’re aiming for **three dinners you don’t have to think about**. If we got **protein in the house** and **one veg side**, would that already be easier than last week? What blocked you — time, groceries, or energy?”

**2-minute downgrade:** Rotisserie chicken + bagged salad + microwave rice = valid meal prep “win.”

## AI / Coach Usage Note

Always ask: **training schedule that week**, **who eats at home**, **allergies**, and **one realistic prep window** before prescribing volumes or macros.
