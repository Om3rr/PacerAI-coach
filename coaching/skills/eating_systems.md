# Skill: Building Simple, Repeatable Eating Systems

**Purpose:** Move parents off constant decision-making onto **anchors + menus + visual portions** so fat-loss phases survive work travel, kids’ activities, and restaurants.

---

## Step-by-Step Process

1. **Name 2–3 anchor meals** (same most weekdays): usually **breakfast**, **lunch**, and optionally **post-training snack**.  
2. **Build a personal menu** of 10–15 go-to options across categories (see template).  
3. **Apply plate method** at flexible meals (dinner, weekends).  
4. **Pre-decide eating-out rules** (3–5 non-negotiables + defaults).  
5. **Review weekly:** one menu swap if boredom; keep anchors stable.

---

## Anchor Meals (Concept + Examples)

**Definition:** Meals eaten **on autopilot** — minimal variation, pre-shopped, emotionally easy.

| Anchor slot | Why it matters for parents | Examples |
|-------------|---------------------------|----------|
| Breakfast | Sets glucose/satiety for chaotic mornings | Greek yogurt + berries + hemp; eggs + toast + fruit; protein smoothie template |
| Lunch | Work/cafeteria/takeout risk zone | “Salad + double protein + dressing on side”; leftovers box; bento: deli meat + veg + fruit |
| Post-training | Stops evening rebound eating | Chocolate milk + banana; sandwich; yogurt + granola portion |

**Coach script:**  
> “Which **two meals** could be **the same 5 days a week** without making you miserable? We’ll protect variety at **dinner** instead.”

## Plate Method (No Calorie Counting)

**Visual for fat-loss phases (adjust for heavy aerobic days):**

- **½ plate** non-starchy vegetables (or salad volume equivalent)  
- **¼ plate** lean protein (palm-to-fist thickness — individualize by hunger and training)  
- **¼ plate** smart carbs (potato, rice, beans, fruit — **larger fraction** long/hard training days)  
- **Thumb** of fats (oils, nuts, cheese — easy to over-add when veg is low)

**Coach line:**  
> “We’re not weighing food. We’re **building the plate** so protein and fiber show up **before** the starch does.”

**Aerobic adjustment:** Add **half-plate carb** *or* pre-meal carb snack on key session days — plate method is a **default**, not a religion.

## Eating Out & Ordering In

**Pre-decided rules (pick 3–5 with client):**

1. **Protein first decision:** “I decide entrée by protein source, not by craving picture.”  
2. **Veg side rule:** Salad or double veg instead of fries *unless* it’s a planned higher-carb day.  
3. **Half-plate to-go** or appetizer + side strategy for huge portions.  
4. **Sauce/dressing:** On the side; skip bread basket *or* count it as carb portion.  
5. **Alcohol:** Match with water + protein anchor earlier; cap *before* ordering (see Parent Challenges skill).

**Cuisine defaults (examples):**

- **Mexican:** Bowl, double protein, beans, fajita veg; limit chips *or* budget them.  
- **Italian:** Protein-led dish + side salad; pasta as portion, not endless bowl.  
- **Asian:** Stir-fry / sushi with sashimi or edamame; watch fried + sugary sauces.  
- **Delivery apps:** Save **3 saved meals** in app favorites — removes scroll fatigue.

**Script:**  
> “When you’re tired, **defaults beat discipline**. What are your **three saved orders**?”
